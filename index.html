The Cold And Hot Chick-fil-A

A Poem by K

Whose Chick-fil-A is that? I think I know.
Its owner is quite sad though.
It really is a tale of woe,
I watch him frown. I cry hello.

He gives his Chick-fil-A a shake,
And sobs until the tears make.
The only other sound's the break,
Of distant waves and birds awake.

The Chick-fil-A is cold, hot and deep,
But he has promises to keep,
Until then he shall not sleep.
He lies in bed with ducts that weep.

He rises from his bitter bed,
With thoughts of sadness in his head,
He idolises being dead.
Facing the day with never ending dread.

With thanks to the poet, Robert Frost, for the underlying structure.
